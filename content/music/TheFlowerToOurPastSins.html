The Flower to our Past Sins

"Bloom" is also a cool title

Verse 1 (Peep 1)

We planted seeds, but they grew into weeds,

Dreams we watered got tangled in greed.

Every word was a dagger, sharp like a thorn,

Our garden of love left broken and worn.


I see your shadow in the petals that fall,

Echoes of laughter still haunt these walls.

If I could rewind, just a moment in time,

I'd prune out the poison, and let love shine.

Chorus (Both)

A flower to our past sins, blooming in the pain,

Colors of regret dance in the rain.

If only we could start again, begin,

Would love still thrive, or wilt in the wind?

Verse 2 (Peep 2)

We were flames too wild, burning the air,

Too blind to see the love we shared.

You said you'd stay, but I pushed you away,

Now I’m lost in the words I didn’t say.


Each petal I hold feels heavy with guilt,

A garden of sorrow from the walls we built.

If I could whisper through the cracks of time,

I’d pull back the thorns and call you mine.

Verse 3 (Both)

Now we’re apart, but the seasons still turn,

The lessons of love are the ones that burn.

I hold this rose, it’s a symbol of hope,

That our hearts might heal, find ways to cope.


But even if time won’t give us a shot,

The bloom of regret is all that we’ve got.

A token of sorrow, a piece of our truth,

A flower to sins, and the love of our youth.

Outro (Both)

So I’ll lay this rose where our memories lie,

A silent goodbye, with a tear in my eye.

Our love was a garden, wild and free,

But sometimes beauty is born of tragedy.
